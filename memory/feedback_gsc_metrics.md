GSC data rules for content briefs:

1. GSC data belongs ONLY in the History section. Do not reference GSC metrics anywhere else in the brief.
2. Page-level trend: show monthly clicks and impressions only (last 12 months). No average CTR or average position — those are meaningless across thousands of keywords.
3. Per-keyword comparison: for each anchor/gap keyword from the keyword universe, show current metrics (clicks, impressions, CTR, avg position) vs 3 months ago. This is where CTR and position belong — at the individual keyword level.
4. No MQLs section — removed, not needed.
5. Keywords section uses Ahrefs data (volume, KD, position). SERP section uses Ahrefs SERP data. These sections never reference GSC.

**Why:** George pointed out that page-level average CTR is misleading (dragged down by long-tail noise), GSC data was leaking into other sections where Ahrefs is the right source, and MQLs are not needed.

**How to apply:** Every content brief. History section is the only place for GSC data.
