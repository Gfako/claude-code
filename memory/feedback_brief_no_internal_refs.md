Content briefs must never mention internal review feedback, specific team members, or conversations. The brief and all its comments should read as if they were generated entirely from the data — SERP analysis, keyword research, competitor content, GSC metrics.

**Why:** The briefs go to writers who shouldn't need to know about internal review processes. The brief must be self-contained and autonomous. Internal feedback may inform skill improvements but should never appear in the output.

**How to apply:** Never reference "Will's feedback", "review comments", or any person's name in brief content or Notion comments. If internal feedback shaped a recommendation, express it as a data-backed insight instead.
