# LLM Brief: Sequence 1 — Niche Edit Outreach

## Who We Are

Synthesia is an AI video platform used by 50,000+ companies. One of our features is a free AI video translator that translates any video into 130+ languages with voice cloning (the speaker's original voice is preserved) and automatic lip sync. The tool page is: https://www.synthesia.io/features/video-translator

## What We're Trying to Achieve

We want backlinks to the Synthesia video translator page from relevant blog articles. A backlink is when another website links to our page from within their content. This helps our SEO — more quality backlinks = higher rankings in Google.

## Who We're Emailing

We've identified 120 blog articles where there is a **specific section** that discusses a topic related to video translation. For each prospect, we have:

- **Their name** (first_name) — the article author or content editor
- **Their article topic** (article_topic) — what the article is about (e.g. "multilingual onboarding", "content localization strategy", "video marketing for global audiences")
- **A specific section quote** (section_quote) — a sentence or paragraph from their article where our link would naturally fit (e.g. "Plain text is the easiest to localize. Images are harder, and media assets like videos or podcasts are the most expensive and difficult.")
- **Our target URL** (synthesia_url) — https://www.synthesia.io/features/video-translator
- **Suggested anchor text** (suggested_anchor_text) — what the link text should ideally be (e.g. "AI video translator", "translate training videos into multiple languages")

These people are typically content managers, blog editors, SEO managers, or marketing leaders at SaaS/tech companies. They receive link requests regularly, so our pitch needs to stand out.

## The Offer / Value Exchange

In exchange for adding our link to their article, we offer them a **free Synthesia plan for one full year**. This is a real, premium plan (worth several hundred dollars/year) that gives them full access to:

- AI video translator (130+ languages, voice cloning, lip sync)
- AI video generator (text-to-video with AI avatars)
- 230+ AI avatars
- Screen recording + editing
- Video templates and brand kit

This is NOT a fake offer or a trial — it's a genuine full plan. This is our differentiator. Most link building emails offer nothing in return. We're offering a premium tool worth real money.

## Tone & Style Guidelines

- **Casual and human** — write like a real person sending a quick email, not a marketing template
- **Short** — each email should be under 100 words. People don't read long cold emails
- **No corporate speak** — avoid phrases like "I wanted to reach out", "hope this finds you well", "synergy", "leverage"
- **No desperation** — don't oversell. Be confident but not pushy
- **Specific** — reference their exact article and the exact section. This proves we actually read their content and aren't mass-blasting
- **Plain text only** — no HTML formatting, no images, no fancy signatures. Just text and one link
- **One CTA per email** — don't ask for multiple things. Each email should have one clear next step
- **Sign off as "George"** — first name only, casual

## What NOT to Do

- Don't use the words "backlink" or "link building" — frame it as "adding a resource" or "linking to it"
- Don't say "I noticed your article..." — too generic, everyone says this
- Don't start with flattery ("Great article!") — it reads as fake when it's the first thing you say
- Don't mention SEO or ranking benefits — we're framing this as helpful for their readers, not for our SEO
- Don't send the same message in follow-ups — each follow-up should add new information or a new angle
- Don't make the free plan feel like a bribe — frame it as "a thank you" or "since you'd probably find it useful anyway"

## Email Sequence Structure

### Email 1 (Day 0): The Pitch
**Goal:** Get them to consider adding the link. Show we read their article, explain why the link fits, and offer the free plan.

Must include:
- Reference to their article on {{article_topic}} — this is the PRIMARY personalization and always available
- Brief explanation of what Synthesia's video translator does (130+ languages, voice cloning, lip sync)
- The URL to our video translator page
- The free plan offer as a thank you
- A soft question CTA ("Would you be open to this?" / "Worth considering?" / "Let me know if interested")

Optional enhancement:
- If `{{section_quote}}` is available, reference it to make the email more specific ("the part where you mention {{section_quote}}")
- But the email MUST read naturally and make sense even when section_quote is empty

### Email 2 (Day 3): The Value Add
**Goal:** Don't just "bump" — give them a new reason to say yes.

Angle: Expand on what the free plan includes — it's not just a video translator, it's a full AI video platform. They can use it for their own content creation too. This makes the offer more tangible and appealing.

Must include:
- Brief reminder of what you proposed (1 sentence max)
- New information about what the Synthesia plan includes
- Frame it as useful for their own work, not just a reward for adding a link
- Soft CTA

### Email 3 (Day 8): The Easy Close
**Goal:** Make saying yes require zero effort.

Angle: Provide a ready-to-go sentence they can copy-paste into their article. Remove all friction. If they've been thinking about it but haven't acted because it seems like work, this solves that.

Must include:
- A pre-written sentence with the link embedded that they can paste directly into their article — something generic enough to fit any article about {{article_topic}}, like: "Tools like [Synthesia's video translator]({{synthesia_url}}) can handle this automatically with AI voice cloning and lip sync."
- The sentence should feel natural and editorial, not promotional
- Reiterate the free plan offer one last time
- Friendly close — no pressure
- This email removes all friction — they don't need to think about what to write, just paste

## Template Variables (Reply.io format)

Use these exact variable names in double curly braces:
- `{{first_name}}` — their first name (always available)
- `{{article_topic}}` — what their article is about (always available)
- `{{synthesia_url}}` — https://www.synthesia.io/features/video-translator (always available)
- `{{section_quote}}` — a specific section from their article (ONLY available for ~60% of prospects — empty for the rest)
- `{{suggested_anchor_text}}` — the recommended link text (available for most)

**IMPORTANT: 40% of prospects do NOT have a usable section_quote.** The emails MUST work without it. Write the email so that `{{article_topic}}` is the primary personalization, and `{{section_quote}}` adds extra specificity when present. Do NOT make the email dependent on section_quote existing.

Two approaches:
1. If Reply.io supports conditional blocks: use them to show the section quote only when it exists
2. If not: write the email so it references "your article on {{article_topic}}" which always works, and treat the section quote as optional context the sender can manually add before sending

## Output Format

Please write 3 emails, each with:
- Subject line (2 variants for A/B testing on email 1, 1 subject for follow-ups using Re: thread)
- Body (plain text, under 100 words)
- Sign-off: "George"
