# LLM Brief: Sequence 2 — Generic Pitch / Video Creation Outreach

## Who We Are

Synthesia is an AI video platform used by 50,000+ companies. Our platform lets anyone create professional videos using AI avatars — no camera, no studio, no editing skills needed. You type a script, pick an avatar, and get a polished video in minutes. The platform supports 130+ languages.

Key product page: https://www.synthesia.io

## What We're Trying to Achieve

We want to get Synthesia mentioned and linked from relevant blog articles. Our approach here is different from a traditional link request — instead of asking for a link, we're offering to **create a free video for their article** using Synthesia. In exchange, they embed the video and add a simple mention like "Video created with Synthesia" with a link.

This achieves three things:
1. We get a backlink and brand mention from a relevant website
2. They get free professional video content that improves their article
3. Their readers get a better experience (articles with embedded video perform better)

## Who We're Emailing

We've identified 133 blog articles that are **topic-adjacent** to video translation — they cover topics like global workforce management, content localization strategy, multilingual training, international SEO, SaaS global expansion, employee onboarding, etc. These articles don't have a specific section where a video translator link fits naturally, BUT their audience is the right audience for Synthesia.

For each prospect, we have:
- **Their name** (first_name) — the article author or content editor
- **Their article topic** (article_topic) — what the article is about (e.g. "hreflang implementation guide", "remote employee engagement strategies", "SaaS global expansion playbook")
- **Their article URL** (prospect_url) — the page we want to add the video to

These people are typically content managers, blog editors, SEO managers, or marketing leaders at SaaS/tech companies.

## The Offer / Value Exchange

We offer to **create a 1-2 minute professional video** summarizing a key section of their article. This video:

- Features a realistic AI presenter (from Synthesia's library of 230+ avatars)
- Is professionally produced — looks like a studio video, not a cheap animation
- Can be embedded directly in their article via an embed code or hosted link
- Is 100% free — we create it, they use it

**What we ask in return:**
- They embed the video in their article
- They add a brief mention that it was made with Synthesia, with a link to our site
- Something like: "Video created with [Synthesia](https://www.synthesia.io)" — one line

This is a strong offer because:
- Creating a video like this would normally cost them $500-2,000 if done traditionally
- Articles with embedded video get significantly more engagement (time on page, lower bounce rate)
- Google rewards pages with rich media (video) in rankings
- They get premium content for free, we get a mention + link

## Tone & Style Guidelines

- **Casual and human** — write like a real person, not a marketing team
- **Short** — each email under 100 words
- **Generous, not transactional** — lead with what we're GIVING, not what we want. The mention should feel like a tiny ask compared to the value of a free video
- **No corporate speak** — avoid "reach out", "hope this finds you well", "leverage", "synergy"
- **Confident but not pushy** — we're offering something valuable, not begging
- **Specific to their article** — reference their article topic so it doesn't feel like a mass email
- **Plain text only** — no HTML, no images, no fancy formatting
- **Sign off as "George"** — casual, first name only

## What NOT to Do

- Don't mention "backlink" or "link building" — frame it as "a mention" or "credit"
- Don't lead with what you want — lead with what you're offering
- Don't make the video sound low quality — emphasize it's professional, AI-generated, studio-quality
- Don't be vague about what the video will be — say it'll cover a specific section of their article
- Don't oversell the SEO benefits — mention it once as a secondary benefit, not the main pitch
- Don't send the same message in follow-ups — each email should add a new angle
- Don't make the mention/credit feel like a big ask — it's one line at the bottom of the video or article

## Email Sequence Structure

### Email 1 (Day 0): The Offer
**Goal:** Get them excited about receiving free video content for their article.

The psychology here: nobody ever emails a blogger saying "let me create free content for you." They always ask for something. We're flipping the script — leading with generosity. The mention of Synthesia is framed as a small, almost afterthought ask.

Must include:
- Reference to their article topic (shows we're not mass-emailing)
- The offer: we'll create a short professional video summarizing a key section
- What it'll look like: AI presenter, 1-2 minutes, ready to embed
- What we ask: just a mention that it was made with Synthesia
- Turnaround time: "I can have a draft ready in 48 hours"
- Soft CTA: "Want me to put something together?"

### Email 2 (Day 4): The Business Case
**Goal:** Give them a rational reason to say yes, not just an emotional one.

Angle: Articles with embedded video get more engagement. Share a stat about video improving time on page or reducing bounce rate. This helps them justify saying yes — it's not just free content, it's a strategic improvement to their article's performance.

Also make the process feel effortless — offer to pick the section yourself.

Must include:
- A relevant stat about embedded video and article performance
- Offer to choose the best section for the video (so they don't have to think about it)
- Reiterate it's free with just a Synthesia mention
- Soft CTA

### Email 3 (Day 9): The Curiosity Hook
**Goal:** Create a reason for them to reply, even if they've ignored the first two.

Angle: Say you already identified a specific section from their article that would work great as a video and you're happy to share the concept. This creates curiosity — people want to see what you picked. It also shows effort and specificity.

Close with a no-pressure line — the offer stays open if they ever want it.

Must include:
- "I went ahead and identified a section that would work well as a video"
- Curiosity hook: "Happy to share the concept if you're curious"
- No-pressure close
- Brief, friendly sign-off
