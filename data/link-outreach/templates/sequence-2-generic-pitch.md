## Notes

- Email 1: Leads with what we'll DO for them (create a video), not what we want (a mention). The mention is framed as a small ask in exchange for real work.
- Email 2: Adds a business case — SEO benefit of embedded video. Gives them a reason to say yes beyond "it's free." Also makes the process easy ("I can pick one").
- Email 3: Creates urgency by saying we already identified a section. Curiosity hook — "happy to share the concept." Low-pressure close.
- Subject line Variant A ("I made a video") is bolder — implies the work is already done. Variant B is safer. A/B test to see which gets more opens.
- All emails under 80 words. Plain text. No HTML.
- This pitch works because we're offering genuine content, not just asking for a favor. Most people have never been offered free professional video for their blog post.
