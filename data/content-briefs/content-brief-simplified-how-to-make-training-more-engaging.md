## 🚨 Core Pain Points

- Compliance training is the worst offender — readers explicitly search "how to make compliance training fun" (250/mo, highest adjacent volume); they need tactics that work specifically when the subject matter is dull.
- Virtual/remote training has a specific drop-off pattern (cameras off, multitasking, Zoom fatigue) that general advice doesn't address — 150 searches/mo for "virtual" variant.
- L&D can't prove engagement drives behaviour change — most articles stop at completion/NPS; what readers need are measurement tactics that connect to on-the-job behaviour.
- Engagement playbooks assume facilitator-led, synchronous training — readers with async / self-paced / LMS-based programs struggle to apply the same ideas.
- Rolling out engaging training across languages and time zones multiplies effort — no ranking page addresses multilingual engagement.
- "Engagement" gets conflated with "entertainment" — readers fear that chasing engagement means dumbing down content; they want the tradeoffs explained.

## 📝 Content Direction

- Lead with the list. Earn the POV. Don't open with theory — open with an insight ("most training is designed for compliance, not engagement") and then pivot.
- Use stats wherever defensible — Continu's post is less original than Synthesia's, but its stats make it citable. Match that on citability.
- Keep editorial voice (this is Amy Vidor's strength). Don't flatten into listicle banality.
- Cross-link the interactive-training-videos page aggressively — it's the natural next read and has been briefed to push toward examples.
- Make the use-case section genuinely specific — don't repeat the 12-ways list three times. Compliance needs a different angle (scenarios + real consequences); virtual needs a different angle (short, camera-on, interaction every 5 min); safety needs a different angle (simulations, decision trees).
