## 🚨 Core Pain Points

- L&D leaders are under pressure to prove business impact but still default to completion rates and NPS — there's no shared language for "performance-driven" L&D metrics.
- Most L&D functions are perceived as reactive order-takers; leaders don't know how to shift to "strategic partner" status, and don't have a benchmark to see where they currently sit.
- Strategy docs are abundant — what's scarce is turning a strategy deck into a quarterly, resource-loaded roadmap that survives leadership changes and budget cycles.
- Leadership alignment is inconsistent. Most L&D teams meet with execs ad-hoc or yearly; they don't know what cadence "good" looks like.
- AI is reshaping L&D (authoring, personalisation, skills intelligence) faster than strategy docs can keep up — L&D leaders need a way to fold AI into their plan without re-writing everything.
- Scaling training across global/distributed workforces (languages, time zones, regulatory variance) breaks most strategy frameworks designed for single-office orgs.
