## 🚨 Core Pain Points

These are the real pain points the audience has — extracted from the SERP, PAA, and what competitors actually solve for. Use these to drive every section.

- **"I keep reading definitions, not examples."** Most articles bait-and-switch — "examples" in the URL but mostly philosophy in the body. Readers want to see what Toyota / Amazon / Southwest *actually did*.
- **"I need to pitch this internally and the abstract principles don't land."** Operations leaders need vivid case stories with measurable outcomes, not lists of values.
- **"I don't know how to translate Toyota / Six Sigma stories into my non-manufacturing team."** Healthcare, financial services, SaaS, retail readers want examples from *their* industry, not just the same Toyota Production System every page recycles.
- **"How do I measure whether we're actually being operationally excellent?"** PAA and matching keywords show high demand for KPIs / measurement (`how to measure operational excellence`, 50 vol). No top page covers this well.
- **"What's the difference between operational excellence, continuous improvement, lean, and Six Sigma?"** Readers conflate these. Pipefy is the only competitor that disambiguates clearly.

## 🔧 Recommended Changes

- **Structure:**
  - Replace the 25-principle list with ~10 *named-company* examples grouped by industry (Manufacturing, Retail, Aviation, Healthcare, Tech).
  - Move principles/tools (Lean, Six Sigma, Kaizen, TPS, Shingo) into a single concise "frameworks" H2 — not 25 separate H2s.
  - Add a "How to measure operational excellence" H2 with concrete KPIs — nobody owns this.
  - Add an FAQ H2 that captures the four PAA questions verbatim.
- **Content:**
  - For each named example, write a 100–150-word block: company → what they did → which framework/principle → measurable outcome. Vivid > exhaustive.
  - Cut the 25-numbered structure entirely. Don't keep "Example 1, Example 2…" framing.
  - Keep total word count to ~1,800 (vs current 3,500). Match SERP norms.
- **Positioning:**
  - Claim the angle competitors miss: operational excellence in *training & internal comms* (consistent SOPs, multilingual onboarding, change-management videos). One H2 dedicated to this is the Synthesia differentiator.
  - Do not change the URL — preserve ranking signals during the rewrite.
