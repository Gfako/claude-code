# The 15 Best Marketing Software Tools in 2026 (Honest Comparison)

In 2026, there are more marketing software options than ever, and that is part of the problem. There are too many platforms that claim to be the all-in-one solution, that only deliver on a few promises, and charge you on a per-seat basis. Knowing which one you actually need has less to do with which one offers the most bells and whistles and more to do with avoiding any you shouldn't even be considering.

I evaluated more than 50 marketing platforms across eight different categories: email marketing, marketing automation, customer relationship management (CRM), search engine optimization (SEO), social media, video, analytics and artificial intelligence (AI). I've distilled them into the following short list. Here's a breakdown of the categories and what I learned from testing each of the tools.

## How I picked

To be included on this list, each tool had to be in use by at least one marketing team I know, had to have transparent pricing information on the company website, and had to be compatible with at least three other tools that most businesses would already be using as part of their marketing stack. I ruled out any that scored less than four stars on G2, and any that had been acquired or rebranded within the last year but confirmed that the product and its functionality remained the same.

The categories below contain tools that most marketing teams will need to operate their businesses. You may not need every single one, but you should definitely find tools that work within your budget and match the stage that your team is in.

## Email marketing software

Email continues to deliver the highest return on investment of any channel, so naturally it should come at the top of this list. Mailchimp is the go-to for first-time businesses because of its free tier, and because the editor is intuitive and easy to use. If you need email and automation in one, go with ActiveCampaign. For ecommerce businesses, there's Klaviyo because of its native integration with Shopify, and if you are a creator or solo marketer who relies heavily on landing pages and segmentation, use ConvertKit, now called Kit.

Email marketing tools cost as little as free and start around $15 a month, depending on how many contacts you have. (I've worked with dozens of businesses who vastly underestimated how many contacts they would have to maintain.)

## Marketing automation software

Marketing automation tools are the place where a marketing stack succeeds or fails. The most popular is HubSpot because it is an all-in-one tool that covers CRM, email marketing and automation, and that is also where the tool falls apart for some because teams are paying for features that they are not using. ActiveCampaign is more streamlined and gives you more control over your marketing automations, but it may require some getting used to. For budget-minded teams that want SMS included with email, Brevo is the way to go. Marketo is the enterprise standard, but only worth considering at scale.

The biggest concern for most businesses is not which features each offers, but which tools can integrate well with each other. Before signing up with a tool, ask yourself the questions regarding its ability to integrate with your CRM, calendar and form tools.
