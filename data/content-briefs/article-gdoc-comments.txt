Anchored to: "How long should a YouTube video script be?
It depends on the topic and the format, but as a rough guide: explainers and educational videos land best between 5 and 10 minutes, brand and product videos between 90 seconds and 4 minutes, and Shorts under 60 seconds. There is no universal optimal length, but there is an optimal density: every second has to earn its place.

Once you know your target length, you can work backward to a word count. One minute of video is roughly 160 to 180 words at a natural speaking pace, which accounts for pauses and B-roll narration.

Quick reference:

2-minute video: 320 to 360 words
5-minute video: 800 to 900 words
10-minute video: 1,600 to 1,800 words
15-minute video: 2,400 to 2,700 words

These are upper bounds, not targets. If your script comes in lighter, that&#39;s usually a good sign.
