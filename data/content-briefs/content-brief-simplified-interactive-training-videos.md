## 🚨 Core Pain Points

- Passive training videos get skipped, watched at 2x, or forgotten — completion rate doesn't equal retention, and L&D can't prove the training worked.
- Most teams don't know what "interactive" actually means beyond adding a quiz at the end — they need a taxonomy of interactivity types mapped to training goals.
- Existing interactive video tools (H5P, Articulate Storyline) require technical skills and separate authoring environments — disconnected from how L&D teams actually produce video today.
- Updating interactive videos is painful — a single policy change means re-shooting or re-recording, which kills the ROI case.
- Hard to localise interactive training at scale — subtitles and dubbed audio break timing for hotspots, decision points, and quizzes.
- No clear answer on how to measure whether interactivity actually moves the needle (completion vs retention vs behaviour change vs assessment scores).
