# The 15 Best Marketing Software Tools of 2026 (An Honest Comparison)

We have an abundance of choices for our stack in 2026, and this abundance has actually led to a significant problem. It’s a crowded, confusing, and expensive market filled with platforms that are trying to be all things for all users, but often do some of them well and charge by the seat. Choosing your best stack is less about picking a winner and more about picking a loser. I tried over 50 of the best marketing tools from across 8 categories: email, automation, CRM, SEO, social, video, analytics, and AI, and then I whittled the long list down to just a handful that are actually worth using. Here’s my findings.

## How I chose these software vendors

First, for a marketing tool to be on this list, I needed at least one marketing team in my network to be actively using it, I needed to be able to find its pricing listed somewhere on its website, and I needed to be able to see if that tool integrates with at least three other tools in a standard stack. Second, I filtered out any platform with less than a 4-star rating on G2. Third, I fact-checked tools that have been acquired or rebranded in the last year to make sure the tool itself hasn’t really changed at all.

The categories below represent the software I consider essential for most marketing teams to run their day-to-day marketing. If you’re in any stage, pick the ones that fit your budget. You won’t need everything on this list, and you shouldn’t.

## Best Email Marketing Software

Email is consistently the highest ROI marketing channel of all time, so these are the best email platforms to use for your own email marketing:

- Mailchimp is the go-to for early-stage companies because it’s got the best free plan, and it has a simple editor.
- ActiveCampaign is the tool that any team looking to get email and marketing automation in one place will reach for.
- Klaviyo is the best choice for ecommerce marketing tools because it integrates directly into Shopify, and is therefore the best choice for ecommerce businesses.
- ConvertKit, which rebranded to Kit this year, is the leading newsletter and email marketing software choice for creators and solo entrepreneurs that are heavily dependent on landing pages and marketing funnels.

Prices start from free to roughly $15 a month, which increases as you hit contact count thresholds, though many teams under-estimate how fast they can reach these thresholds.

## Best Marketing Automation Software

Marketing automation tools are where many of my marketing stack either comes together or breaks apart:

- HubSpot is the most popular tool because it includes CRM, email marketing, and marketing automation tools in one platform, and many times this means a company can overpay for tools they won’t ever use.
- ActiveCampaign is less powerful and more focused around the automation features, though they also have a steeper learning curve to the interface.
- Brevo is the budget alternative, that combines email marketing tools with SMS in the same flow.
- Marketo is the industry standard for enterprise, but unless you’re in that space, you shouldn’t consider it for your stack.

The real decision factor for me has nothing to do with features and everything to do with what integrations are available; so, check to see how the tool connects with your CRM, your calendar and your form-building tools before committing to a tool.
