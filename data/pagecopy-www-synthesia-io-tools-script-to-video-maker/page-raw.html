What makes a good video script?

A good video script has a clear goal, a defined audience, and a strong structure that makes it easier for your video to engage and convert.

It should start with a hook to grab attention quickly, then move to a compelling message or solution, support that with proof or examples, and conclude with a clear call‑to‑action.

Keep the language conversational and the timing smooth (about 130–150 spoken words per minute), so that narration feels natural and pacing doesn’t lose viewers.

Finally, map visuals and audio side‑by‑side (e.g. using a two-column format). This ensures that what viewers see matches what they hear and makes the final video more coherent.
