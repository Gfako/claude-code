## 🚨 Core Pain Points (audience)

- **Stack sprawl.** Average B2B marketing team runs 8–15 tools and can't tell which ones overlap. They're paying for 3 tools that all "do attribution" and none of them agree.
- **Buyer can't always pick the buyer.** The marketer signs the contract, but Sales / RevOps / IT block the choice. Articles never address this political reality.
- **Pricing opacity at enterprise tier.** Most "marketing automation" tools (Marketo, Eloqua, Pardot) hide pricing behind sales. Buyers want a realistic floor + indicator of what the second-year jump looks like.
- **Sales-marketing alignment.** B2B-specific pain — the marketing tool only matters if it plays nicely with CRM, lead routing, scoring, and the SDR workflow.
- **Localization for global B2B.** Multi-region B2B companies need video, email, and content workflows that can be localized into 5+ languages without rebuilding everything.
- **Enablement gap.** Tools are bought, then sit unused because no one trained the team. The "soft" tooling category (learning, enablement, internal video) is almost always overlooked.

## 📝 Content Direction

- Lead with category framing, not alphabet. B2B buyers think in jobs-to-be-done.
- 18 tools across 10+ categories. Don't load on automation platforms — most competitors do, and the article gets repetitive.
- Real prices in every section. Where prices aren't public, say "contact sales — expect $40k+/yr for mid-market" or similar honest estimates.
- One paragraph per tool: what it does → who it's best for → standout feature → price → pros/cons (bullets).
- First person sparingly: open the article in first person ("In my work across B2B marketing teams…") and sprinkle 4–5 more first-person hooks across tool sections. Don't make every tool a "personal experience."
- Synthesia section MUST focus on the **video translator** — angle: localized B2B campaigns into 5+ languages without re-shooting.
- 5app section: position as the modern enablement platform — fits "your team bought the tool, now they actually need to use it."
- Constant Contact: position for **service-based B2B** and small consultancies — not enterprise.
