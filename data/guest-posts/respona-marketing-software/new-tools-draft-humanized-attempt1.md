1. **Semrush: One-stop shop for SEO and paid search analysis**

If Ahrefs is your primary SEO and paid research tool, then Semrush is its closest competitor, and the one I turn to when a client wants to pair their organic search work with an all-out content and paid search solution. If the team you serve also runs Google Ads, Semrush’s content marketing workflows are more robust than Ahrefs, and the Position Tracking tool has the cleanest interface of any I’ve tested. Many SEO and paid marketers will opt for Semrush because they need to do their paid ads research on the same platform as organic. Prices start at $139.95 a month for the basic Pro tier.

2. **Wistia: Marketing-focused video platform with built-in analytics**

Wistia is a video hosting platform with features and marketing analytics that are tailor-made for marketers, including lead capture forms, heatmaps, and engagement scoring. Its marketing analytics are cleaner than any I’ve come across, and there are far more options for customizing the video player than there are with standard YouTube embeds. Video will eventually be a critical lead generation channel for marketing teams. Wistia is the video host I see most marketers eventually adopt. A free tier is available to smaller marketing teams, and paid plans start at $24 a month.

3. **Zendesk: Full-service customer service ticketing for email, chat, and web**

Zendesk is the gold standard for customer service ticketing across email, chat, and web. It’s a great way to complement a phone-first CX platform like CloudTalk and provide a streamlined customer experience that covers your other contact channels. Marketing teams involved end-to-end in the customer journey should be looking at Zendesk to serve as the backbone for their customer service department. Zendesk even has reporting available that can help support marketing teams by funneling data on customer service issues into content marketing and product marketing efforts. Pricing starts at $19 per agent per month.

4. **Hotjar: Qualitative analytics through heatmaps and session recordings**

Hotjar injects qualitative analysis into a marketing stack that otherwise relies on quantitative tools like Google Analytics. Its heatmaps, scroll maps, and session recordings provide deep insights into exactly how users move through websites, information that quantitative data will never tell you. If you’re optimizing a landing page, Hotjar helps answer “Why did my conversion rate drop?” It’s available for free for smaller marketing teams, with paid plans starting at $32 a month.
