# 2026’s Top 20 Marketing Software Options: A Short List

In 2026, there are more marketing software choices than ever, yet this has inadvertently become our marketing stack’s biggest issue. There are thousands of digital marketing products fighting over marketing budgets, all of them claiming to simplify your marketing, all of them charging per-seat. Creating a modern digital marketing stack is not about finding the good tools, it is about figuring out which are worth the price and which are holding back your marketing team.

Over the past few years, I have been reviewing marketing software for in-house marketing teams, marketing growth consulting firms, and bootstrapped small business marketers. I have reviewed over 100 marketing tools, paid pilots on dozens of marketing apps, and seen countless marketing software tools quietly get put on the back shelf for not integrating well with the rest of the digital marketing stack. The following are the marketing tools that made the cut. They serve the digital marketing functions most modern-day digital marketers need, including email marketing, marketing automation, marketing analytics, SEO, content marketing, content management, customer support, video marketing, and project management.

## How I chose the marketing tools in this list

Marketing tools here had to clear three hurdles: 1) at least one of the marketing teams I work with uses the tool for its marketing functions in real-world situations; 2) pricing is published on the website, not hidden behind a demo; and 3) the tool has been shown to integrate well with three other digital marketing tools. Anything below a four-star rating on G2 was discarded, as were apps that have been bought and renamed since last year (I did check to see if they work the same way they did before).

The following 20 marketing tools serve the marketing functions that most marketing teams need to market. You likely do not need all of these. Select the ones that align with your company size, budget, and digital marketing strategy for this marketing quarter.

## The 20 Best Marketing Tools to Use in 2026

### 1. Respona is the best for Digital PR and Outreach

I continue to return to Respona for digital PR and outreach marketing. Respona is a tool for automated email prospecting and contact research that unifies prospect searching, email checking, and outreach in one workflow. Provide the tool with a simple query, it finds prospects for you and double-checks all of its email data for accuracy, and you can send personalized email campaigns without the "spray and pray" style of generic outreach tools. If you run any sort of content marketing program at speed, it will pay for itself in just a few campaigns, and I generally find content marketers using Respona as digital marketing infrastructure rather than a campaign tool. It also supports outreach activities for broken link building, affiliate recruitment, and influencer outreach.

2. **Ahrefs — SEO research and analytics platform**
Ahrefs is the go-to SEO software used by digital marketers for keyword research, backlink research, and competitor research. While it is a fairly dense tool, it offers top-notch data quality and the Ahrefs dashboard maps keyword opportunities directly to real page rankings, making SEO data useful for content marketing. Priced at $129 for the basic level, the cost is minimal to any digital marketing program for which organic search is a primary growth channel. And Ahrefs integrates easily into most content marketing and content management software.

3. **Synthesia — AI video and localization**
Synthesia is the most popular AI video production platform for marketers. The user types in a video script, selects an AI avatar, and Synthesia produces a full video with matching lips and voiceover in over 140 languages. For digital marketing teams needing to localize a single marketing campaign for various markets, or for those needing to generate large volumes of training and product video without a production team, it is the best platform on the market. More than half of the Fortune 100 use Synthesia precisely because of that. If you are a digital marketer who has been unable to implement video marketing as a channel for your company due to prohibitive cost, this tool allows you to finally do so. It also acts as a central repository for managing video assets across global markets, which will become essential to large enterprise content marketing.

4. **Loom — Quick screen recordings**
Loom is best for fast, short screen recording. Great for product marketing demos, client success videos, and asynchronous team communication within a marketing department. It’s also an excellent lightweight add-on to a comprehensive AI video marketing tool like Synthesia. Marketers rely on it each and every day for prospect outreach and internal announcements.

### 5. Constant Contact — Best email marketing tool for small business owners

If I could pick one email marketing tool that I recommend most to small business owners and service-based marketers, it’s this one. Constant Contact was designed with the real-world challenges of running email campaigns without a dedicated marketing team in mind. It’s got user-friendly templates, an uncluttered editor, email marketing automation software that doesn’t take a manual to configure, and the functionality for SMS marketing and event marketing baked right in. And unlike most other email marketing tools, its marketing analytics are straightforward in calling out which email campaign has been a success, and which is struggling, a trait that is pretty unusual as of 2026.

If you’re a small business marketer and you need to launch your email campaigns this week, look no further. This is where you start.

### 6. Mailchimp — Best free email marketing tier

Mailchimp is the most common email marketing software that early-stage marketers go for, simply because of its robust free plan and polished email marketing editor. It grows well to 10,000 contact points, after which the pricing starts to feel steep and the marketing teams will generally make their way to ActiveCampaign or some other dedicated marketing automation solution.

### 7. ActiveCampaign — Best for email marketing and marketing automation

This is the best marketing software choice for marketing teams that want email marketing and marketing automation software in the same platform, with more segmentation options than Mailchimp's free plan allows. It takes a bit more than HubSpot when it comes to learning its marketing automation software, but the time it takes pays off in the ability to fine-tune the specific automation flow you're creating.

### 8. HubSpot Marketing Hub — Best marketing automation software

For good reason, HubSpot Marketing Hub is probably the most used marketing automation software. It bundles CRM, email marketing, marketing automation tools, content marketing, content management, and marketing analytics in one package. That’s all well and good, but the problem is that some marketing teams move past it in terms of the marketing automation software features they actually need. For most marketing teams under 50 employees, this all-in-one solution makes the most sense, as the integration depth alone is worth the extra expense. It costs $20 to get your foot in the door, then the more marketing contacts you add, the more it’ll cost you.

### 9. Marketo Engage — Best marketing automation software for enterprises

Marketo Engage is still the gold standard when it comes to enterprise marketing automation, though it is only necessary for marketing departments that work with enough marketing data to require it in order. Their marketing automation tools support multi-touch campaign management, account-based marketing, and all of the integrations that you’d see from enterprise-level marketing tools. Initial configuration is a bit of a hassle, and the pricing reflects an enterprise-level product.

### 10. Brevo — Most affordable marketing automation and SMS marketing

Brevo is an affordable automation marketing tool that allows you to handle SMS marketing and email marketing in the same funnel. It is a good choice for marketers who need marketing automation tools, but not an enterprise-level pricetag. The free plan is suitable for basic use cases, the paid plans start at $9 a month. Additionally, pricing for the SMS marketing component is competitive.

### 11. Salesforce Marketing Cloud — Best enterprise marketing platform

Salesforce Marketing Cloud is the go-to marketing automation tool for any marketing team on a large scale, particularly when the marketing cloud has to cover multiple business units and/or multiple global regions. It requires an extensive configuration effort to get set up properly, but once set up correctly, it can handle intricate customer engagement and campaign management processes to the extent that no other marketing automation platform can. The Pardot Engagement Studio, which is a part of the Salesforce Marketing Cloud, is a B2B marketing automation tool that's used by enterprise marketing teams for B2B nurturing.

### 12. HubSpot CRM — Best free customer relationship management tool

HubSpot CRM is the free CRM tool that most marketing teams start with. HubSpot CRM integrates with HubSpot Marketing Hub, it is a full featured CRM tool. It has a free version that provides enough customer data, customer behavior tracking, and reporting for marketing teams up to about 100 contacts a week. And if you need to go up a level and switch to HubSpot Marketing Hub, the upgrade process is pretty streamlined.

### 13. CloudTalk — Best customer service and call analytics software

CloudTalk is the CRM tool I recommend most frequently for marketing aware organizations that also need a phone and call center solution with their digital customer service channels. CloudTalk is a cloud-based calling solution built specifically for sales and customer service teams, and it has a call center feature with the ability to click-to-call, record the conversation, and view comprehensive call performance metrics with the purpose of marketing optimization. In case you are a B2B marketer who needs to track marketing campaign impact in inbound calls, CloudTalk will have an integration that allows you to track that accurately. In addition, the CloudTalk reporting tool can give you the ability to track how your marketing campaign contributes to the number of qualified customer interactions, which is the ability to know what works and what doesn't for your marketing.

### 14. CrowdSpring — Best for on-demand creative work and design services

CrowdSpring is a design services marketplace that I recommend most for marketing agencies when a marketing team needs some design services but does not have a full in-house graphic designer. It's a community of highly qualified designers, the platform is built to manage design contests for logos, branding, packaging, and web design. All it takes is a single post to submit a project description to get dozens of design submissions back from designers working live projects to select the best one. For a small business or early marketing stage team who needs brand-quality creative without building an in-house team, it's the cleanest way to get there.

### 15. Canva — Best DIY design tool for marketers

Canva is the day-to-day creative marketing tool most marketers default to for social graphics, presentations, and ad creative. It is the right tool for when your marketing team needs to ship a marketing asset today and doesn't have a custom designer in the budget.

### 16. Google Analytics 4 — Best free marketing analytics

Google Analytics 4 is the free default and foundation of most digital marketing analytics stacks. It's got a steeper learning curve than the prior version, but the data quality is unmatched at the price point. Pair it with Google Search Console for search-side analytics and you have most digital marketing measurement covered. Google Ad campaign attribution flows directly into Google Analytics 4, so marketers can close the loop on paid campaigns inside the same analytics tool already powering their digital marketing reporting.

### 17. Mixpanel — Best product-led marketing analytics

Mixpanel is the marketing analytics alternative product-led marketing teams prefer because it tracks customer behavior and feature usage more granularly than Google Analytics. Marketers running activation and retention marketing campaigns inside a SaaS product use Mixpanel to attribute behavior back to marketing efforts at the cohort level.

### 18. Asana — Best project management tools for marketing teams

Asana is the project management tools default for most marketing teams. It handles marketing tasks across multiple marketing campaigns, integrates with the rest of your marketing stack, and scales from a five-person marketing team to a hundred-person marketing org without a redesign. For most marketers, picking project management tools is less about which is the best platform and more about which will the marketing team actually use consistently.

### 19. Buffer — Best simple social media marketing software

Buffer is the simplest of the social media schedulers and the right pick for solo marketers and small marketing teams. It handles posting, basic analytics, and approval workflows across all the major social platforms. Pricing starts at $6 per month, per channel.

### 20. Sprout Social — Best premium social media management software

Sprout Social is the premium pick with the best analytics for marketing teams that actually use the data. The reporting is the cleanest in the category, and the social listening features integrate with the rest of your marketing automation platform stack in a way Buffer cannot match. If you have a sizeable marketing team running social as a legitimate growth channel, Sprout Social is your upgrade. For marketers who still need design control and approval workflows on email campaign tooling, Campaign Monitor still rates as a credible alternative option.

## How to pick marketing software for your team

Three guidelines should cover 90% of your marketing software buying decisions.

First, integration depth wins out over feature depth. A marketing tool that has 80% of the features that integrates perfectly with the rest of your marketing stack will perform better than a 100% feature-rich tool that requires custom integration. Marketing automation tools, CRMs, and marketing analytics tools are particularly sensitive to this guideline.

Second, real-world pricing beats pricing on a website. Ask every vendor for a price quote that accounts for the seats, integrations, and overages you will actually use over the next 12 months. Vendor pricing pages are best-case scenarios. Marketing automation software pricing in particular is notorious for skyrocketing once you hit specific contact thresholds.

Third, your user should be your buyer. If the person who signs the check is not the marketer who uses the marketing tool every day, the implementation struggles. Have the marketer who owns the marketing campaigns in question lead the product evaluation. This applies especially to email marketing software, marketing automation platform decisions, and CRMs.

## Common mistakes when buying marketing software

The most expensive mistakes that I see marketing teams make tend to be structural. Buying two different marketing tools that solve the same problem in two different ways. Paying for unused seats because no one audited your list of users this year. Signing an annual contract on marketing software before validating that your marketing team is actually going to use it. Switching marketing automation platforms in the middle of a quarter without budgeting for a data migration. Treating your marketing analytics as an afterthought instead of a fundamental foundation of every marketing strategy decision.

If you remember only one takeaway from this list of best marketing software, remember this: the biggest cost line item is never the software. It's the marketing campaign that didn't happen because there was a bad marketing tool in the way.

## FAQ: Marketing software

### What is marketing software?

Marketing software is any tool that can help a marketer or a marketing team plan, implement, measure, or scale their marketing. It encompasses many marketing tools including email marketing, marketing automation, marketing analytics, content marketing, SEO, social media, customer support, and project management. Today, a marketing stack typically uses eight to 12 categories of marketing software at any given time.

### Which marketing automation tool is best for small businesses?

For the small business marketer, the ideal marketing automation software typically marries email marketing, basic CRM, and rudimentary automation. HubSpot Marketing Hub and ActiveCampaign are by far the most common solutions that fit this description. If you're strapped for cash, look at Brevo.

### What is the price of marketing software?

It really depends. Email marketing solutions start free and go all the way up to thousands of dollars a month at the highest tier. Marketing automation software generally starts around $20/mo for solo marketers and goes up to the thousands/mo for enterprise marketing teams. Always have the sales rep run you a pricing plan that has been adjusted for annual payment, overages, and extra seats.

### Do I need separate tools or all-in-one marketing platforms?

This one has no universal right answer. All-in-one marketing suites like HubSpot Marketing Hub and Salesforce Marketing Cloud save you on integration costs at the expense of paying for features you won't use. Conversely, an unbundled "best-of-breed" tool stack gives you superior tools in any given category, but you will have to actually get those tools to integrate properly. Teams below the size of 20 marketers are better off with an all-in-one solution; larger marketing teams almost always end up with a hybrid.

### Can I run my affiliate marketing program in the same tool stack?

Yes. Most current marketing automation suites and email marketing software provide for affiliate marketing tracking and partner commission payments through either native integrations with dedicated affiliate platforms. The marketing automation software you're going to be using for email marketing campaigns is also the ideal space to maintain communications with your marketing affiliates.

### Which is the best free marketing software?

Google Analytics 4, Google Search Console, HubSpot CRM, Mailchimp free plan, Canva free plan, and Trello can serve as your entire marketing tool stack for zero cost. None of these tools are leaders in their field, but for the marketer working for a small business or solo practitioner, these are more than adequate to operate a legitimate marketing department.

## Time to upgrade your marketing software?

Pick the marketing software products that apply to your existing marketing strategy. Pick up the others when your campaigns need them. The best marketing tool stack in 2026 isn't necessarily the one with the most marketing products. It is the one which allows your marketing team to consistently produce, truthfully measure, and meaningfully improve upon its quarterly results.
