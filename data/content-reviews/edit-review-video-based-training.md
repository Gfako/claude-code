- [PARTIAL] **Core pain point: "Will video training actually work?"** — The draft explains cognitive load theory and cites one stat (52% of L&D teams using AI for video, from AI in L&D Report 2026). But the brief specifically asked for retention rates, completion rates, and cost comparisons to build a business case. These are missing. -> Add 2-3 concrete data points: e.g. Forrester's claim that employees are 75% more likely to watch a video than read a document, or Brandon Hall research on video training completion rates vs. text-based. The brief flagged this as a moat opportunity.
- [PARTIAL] **Core pain point: "Training goes stale"** — Addressed in the challenges section and "Why this is changing" with AI benefits. But it's only ~2 sentences. -> Expand with a concrete example: "We had a compliance module that needed updating 3 times in 6 months. With traditional production that meant re-recording each time. - [PARTIAL] **Core pain point: Global/multilingual training** — Mentioned in challenges and AI benefits, plus the Orange case study. But treated as a bullet point, not a substantive section. The brief flagged this as "completely absent from top results" and a key Synthesia differentiator. -> Give localization its own subsection or a dedicated paragraph with specifics (how many languages, what the workflow looks like, what Orange actually achieved).
- [PARTIAL] **Core pain point: Engagement** — The intro references competing for attention, but there's no dedicated section on engagement strategies. - [PARTIAL] **Internal links** — The draft mentions "AI tools like this video generator" and includes a template embed, but there are no visible internal links to the 12+ related pages the brief identified. -> Add links to at minimum: `/post/training-videos`, `/post/employee-training-software`, `/post/interactive-video`, `/post/video-localization`, `/post/cost-of-video-production`, `/features/ai-video-generator`, `/features/avatars`. - [PARTIAL] **Cannibalization resolution** — The brief flagged that Google routes "video based training" to `/learn/training-videos` instead of this page. - [FLAG] **"Why this is changing" repeats the pattern again** — Four AI benefits, same bold + one-line format. Three consecutive sections with identical structure is a red flag.

- [PARTIAL] **"That's where video-based learning has potential"** — Slightly generic connector. -> Something more specific: "That's why the teams I work with have shifted toward video — not because it's trendy, but because it meets people where they already are."
- [DONE] **No major AI cliches detected** — No "In today's fast-paced world," no "Let's dive in," no "comprehensive guide," no "Moreover/Furthermore." Clean.

## Top 3 Priority Actions

1. **Add quantitative evidence and cost data.** The brief's biggest ask was data to help L&D teams build a business case — retention stats, cost comparisons (traditional vs. AI video production), engagement numbers. This is the single biggest gap. Add 3-5 credible data points and a cost comparison paragraph. Link to `/post/cost-of-video-production`.

2. **Add embedded content: FAQ section, YouTube videos, screenshots, case study embed.** The draft is text-heavy with only one template and one table. Add: (a) 5+ FAQs from the brief's suggestions, (b) 1-2 Synthesia YouTube videos, (c) 2-3 product screenshots in the creation workflow section, (d) formal Orange case study embed. This will significantly improve engagement, AEO, and visual appeal.

3. **Break the repetitive bullet pattern in benefits/challenges/AI sections.** Three consecutive sections use the same bold-label + one-sentence format. Vary the structure: turn some into short anecdotes, expand others into full paragraphs, use a different format for each section. This will reduce AI-detection risk and improve readability.
