## C. AI Pattern Analysis

```
AI Pattern Instances Found: 6-7
Severity: Low-Medium (4-8 instances)

Structural issues: 5 — Strategy sections follow similar template (intro → bullets → callout example)
Phrasing issues: 1-2 — Closing paragraphs are vague
Tone issues: Minor — personality throughout offsets most patterns

Highest-risk sections:
1. Strategy callout examples (all follow situation → problem → solution → result)
2. Closing paragraphs ("And AI is giving us the opportunity to be more innovative")
```

### Structural patterns

**5 strategy sections follow a similar template:** Each has an intro paragraph with first-person anecdote → bullet list of tactics → callout example box. This is less rigid than the 14-block repetition in the "training engagement" article, and the first-person anecdotes vary each section, but the callout examples are formulaic.

**Callout example pattern:** All 5 examples follow the exact same 4-paragraph structure:
1. A team does X (situation)
2. Problem description
3. The team changes to Y (solution)
4. Result

This is a minor AI signal — the structure is sound storytelling (situation-complication-resolution) but the identical paragraph count and rhythm across all 5 makes it mechanical.

**Suggested fix:** Vary 2-3 of the example callouts. One could be a named customer case study (DuPont, Modern Canada). One could be a before/after comparison with numbers. One could be a "From experience" first-person anecdote rather than a generic team example.

### Phrasing patterns

Flagged instances:
- "And AI is giving us the opportunity to be more innovative in how we design and deliver exceptional learning experiences" — corporate AI-hype filler
- "Just five years ago, personalized learning at scale wasn't sustainable for most enterprise L&D teams. Now it is." — vague claim without evidence

Em dashes: ~8-10 across ~3,500 words = ~2.5 per 1,000 words. Within acceptable range.

Formulaic transitions: Minimal. The article avoids "Moreover," "Furthermore," etc. This is good.

### Tone patterns

The article has genuine personality throughout. The writer's voice is distinct — dry humor ("more Courbet than Picasso," "we're in the wrong business," "organizing an afternoon boba run"), direct acknowledgment of reality ("I get it. I've dealt with all of these"), and honest framing ("I can't promise you that bringing AI into your administrative workflows will save the cost of a full-time role"). This offsets the structural repetition in the strategy sections.

The closing is the weakest point — it reverts to generic AI optimism that doesn't match the pragmatic tone of the rest of the article.
