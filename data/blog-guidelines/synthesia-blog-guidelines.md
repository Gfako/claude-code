## 1. Writing High Quality Content

### Overview

Google's two main content guideline documents:
1. [Google's general helpful content guidelines](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content)
2. [Google's review guidelines](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/specialty/ecommerce/write-high-quality-reviews)

**4 main content types:**
1. **How-to content:** Explains to the reader how to do something
2. **Review/Listicles:** Provides a list of the top X in Y category
3. **Data-led:** Analyses unique data or statistics
4. **Synthesia-focused content** (we don't touch this)

### First-hand Experience and Expertise

We want content that uses first-hand experience and expertise to provide original information and insights so that our content provides much more value than our competition.

For a how-to post, we want content that:
- Is written by someone who has experience in a real-world L&D situation (and we communicate that to establish trust)
- Is written for a clear audience (L&D, enablement, or training teams), not a generic reader
- Clearly explains *who the article is for* and *what problem it helps solve*
- Includes lots of real-world insights from actual experience
- Shares personal examples of what worked, what didn't, and what they'd do differently
- **Explains *why* certain decisions were made, not just *what* to do. Show the reasoning chain.**
- Goes beyond surface-level tips with practical, experience-based advice
- Provides step-by-step guidance/workflows (using Synthesia) that someone could realistically follow
- Answers common, real questions learners and stakeholders ask
- Adds original insights rather than summarising what's already been said elsewhere
- Focuses on helping the reader succeed
- Includes concrete examples, scenarios, or anecdotes from real projects
- Builds trust by showing hands-on experience and credibility
- Leaves the reader feeling confident they can start themselves

### Guiding Principles for L&D Content

**Two content intents:**
- **Foundational content:** shapes judgment, defines standards, builds trust with enterprise leaders over time
- **Utility content:** captures demand, solves immediate tasks, supports discoverability

**Blog purpose:**
- Introduce Synthesia to new audiences and demonstrate how it addresses real organizational challenges
- Build trust with customers and potential customers

**The Core Reframe — Organizational Problems, Not Use Cases:**

L&D blog posts lead with underlying problems rather than formats, features, or outputs:
1. **Message Drift:** same message loses clarity as it spreads across teams/regions
2. **Bottlenecked Readiness:** preparing people depends on few experts or manual effort
3. **Adoption Gaps:** behaviors fail to stick as work and priorities change
4. **Overloaded Upskilling:** skill development layered on top of work increases fatigue
5. **Loss of Human Connection:** scale/distance strips communication of presence and trust
6. **Short-Lived Capability:** knowledge lives in individuals rather than systems

**Our Vocabulary:**
- **Capability building:** long-term organizational ability to perform and adapt
- **L&D:** the function responsible for developing people at scale
- **Training:** structured skill and knowledge acquisition
- **Enablement:** ongoing support for performance and adaptation
- **Readiness:** confidence and correctness at launch
- **Adoption:** sustained behavior change over time

**Editorial Principles:**
- We strengthen judgment (evaluate trade-offs, not prescribe tactics)
- We design for systems, not tools
- We treat upskilling as strategic
- We lead with empathy and human judgment
- We build a coherent body of thought

### Review/Listicle Content

14 important review posts. These are BoF/MoF topics driving conversions and AI search citations.

Google's review guidelines require:
- Hands-on testing evidence
- Detailed analysis of how tools compare (not just feature lists)
- Screenshots, test results, specific observations
- Quantitative measurements where possible

### Two Reader Personas
- **ICP:** L&D professionals looking for unique insights and expert analysis (higher $ value)
- **General audience:** Less sophisticated readers needing simplified instructions (needed to rank well)

## 2. Content Creation Workflow

### Step 1: Content Brief
- Auto-generated briefs outlining optimal structure, assets, embedded content, data
- Created by SEO team, discussed with SME author

### Step 2: Write/Generate

**What we're aiming for:**
- Clear, useful, grounded in real expertise
- Adds new value (not a summary of existing content)
- Feels human and natural, not generic or templated
- Contains embedded content from the brief

**Using AI:**
- Use AI for structure, ideation, or initial draft
- Final content must reflect your experience, judgment, perspective
- If the content could have been written by anyone, it's not ready
- **Final content must not read as though generated by AI**

**Information gain:**
- The amount of new, useful, or unique value your content adds beyond what already exists
- Include: specific insights, practical explanations, trade-offs/nuances/opinions, concrete examples
- Avoid: generic best practices, high-level summaries, rewriting without adding insight

**Strong draft characteristics:**
- Clear structure with purpose and logical flow
- Specific and concrete with examples
- Reader-focused with clear takeaways
- Demonstrates real expertise
- Natural tone (reads like a human)
- Concise (no filler, repetition, unnecessary length)

**Voice:** First-person at all times

**Final sense check:**
- Does this add new value/information?
- Does it follow the brief's structure, intent, and search optimization?
- Is it clear and easy to follow?
- Does it reflect real experience or expertise?
- Would a reader finish this and feel they learned something useful?

### Step 3: Editing (3-Pass System)

#### Pass 1: Preserve and Understand
- Read the full piece once without editing
- Identify: core argument, first-hand experience, unique insights, generic/vague/templated sections
- Highlight anything lacking specificity or reader value

#### Pass 2: Humanize and Strengthen
- Work paragraph by paragraph
- Rewrite unnatural or generic phrasing
- Increase specificity, ask for additional examples
- Improve flow and transitions
- Remove filler and weak language
- Apply transformations: Generic -> Specific, Formal -> Natural, Abstract -> Concrete, Predictable -> Varied
- Preserve and surface first-hand experience

#### Pass 3: Tighten and Validate
- Cut repetition and unnecessary filler
- Check: one idea per paragraph, logical flow, clear headers, consistent tone
- **Run through AI detection (Originality.ai)**
  - If score > 50%: increase variation, replace templated phrasing, add specificity, break predictable patterns
- Final: Does it feel written by someone with real experience? Adds value? Clear and engaging?

### Step 4: Publish
- Final check by writer and/or SEO team
- Upload to Webflow, update all embedded content
- Check internal links going to and from the post

## 3. Editing Checklist

### Core Principle
- Preserve original meaning, insight, and expertise
- Do not remove or dilute first-hand experience, examples, or opinions
- Edit how something is said, not what is being said
- Improve clarity, usefulness, and readability

### People-first and Value
- Write to help a real reader, not to rank
- Ensure the reader leaves with a clear takeaway
- Each section should add meaningful value beyond existing content
- Avoid summarizing without adding insight
- Remove or improve anything that doesn't help the reader

### Expertise and Credibility
- Preserve and highlight real experience and expertise
- Keep concrete examples, results, and observations
- Ensure claims are grounded, specific, and not overstated
- Make it clear the author understands the topic

### Introductions and Hooks
- Open with a natural, relevant hook
- Use light narrative or real-world context where helpful
- Keep it concise and transition quickly into the topic

### Voice and Tone
- First-person at all times
- Keep shifts intentional and contained
- Write clearly and directly in a natural, human tone

### Humanize and De-genericize
- Replace generic or abstract language with concrete, specific phrasing
- Add examples where needed to make points tangible
- Allow for natural phrasing and light personality
- Avoid overly polished or formulaic writing

### Remove AI Patterns
- Remove templated phrasing and filler openings
- Use em dashes sparingly
- Break up repetitive or overly balanced sentence structures

### Clarity and Structure
- Start sections with the main point (BLUF)
- Keep one idea per paragraph
- Ensure logical flow between paragraphs and sections
- Use clear, descriptive headers with parallel structure
- Follow point -> explanation -> example where possible
- Define jargon clearly

### Sentence Quality and Tightening
- Vary sentence length and structure
- Avoid repetitive sentence openings
- Remove repetition and unnecessary filler
- Cut words where it improves clarity without losing nuance

### Final Checks
- Does this add original value and insight?
- Does it demonstrate real experience or expertise?
- Does it feel human and enjoyable to read?
- Is it clear, specific, and easy to follow?
- Would the reader feel satisfied and not need to search again?
- Would this feel credible and worth sharing?

## 4. AEO Tips

Based on Kevin Indig's research:

- **Front-load key insight in first 30% of page** — 44.2% of citations come from top 30% of content; 10-20% page depth is the peak citation zone
- **Place most citable paragraphs early** — highest citation likelihood from paragraphs within first ~20% of page
- **Don't force answers into first sentence** — 53% of citations come from middle of paragraphs vs. 24.5% from first sentences
- **Open with clear declarative statement ("X is Y")** — citation winners use definitive language 2x more often (36.2% vs. 20.2%); +14% aggregate citation lift
- **Use question-based headings with direct answers** — content with questions ~2x more likely to be cited (18% vs. 8.9%); 78.4% of question-based citations come from headings
- **Increase entity density** — cited content averages 20.6% entity density vs. ~5-8% normal text
- **Include numbers and dates in intro** — DATE and NUMBER are most consistent positive signals
- **Avoid leading with pricing** — PRICE is strongest negative signal in 5 of 6 verticals
- **Write in balanced, analytical tone** — cited content clusters around ~0.47 subjectivity score
- **Keep writing simple and structured** — high-performing content averages Flesch-Kincaid 16 vs. 19.1 for low-performing
- **Build comprehensive, multi-intent pages** — top 4.8% of URLs are category-level guides covering multiple intents
- **Aim for sufficient content length** — pages above ~20,000 characters average 10.18 citations vs. 2.39 under 500
- **Structure content for your vertical** — heading count performance varies; 3-4 headings perform worse than zero
- **Focus on specific, niche entities** — high-cited pages contain fewer Knowledge Graph entities (specificity > general authority)
- **Design content like a structured briefing, not narrative** — AI favors BLUF-style delivery

**Other AEO tips:**
- Update posts regularly
- Include research and original data — models look for evidence

## 5. Internal Linking

**Links TO a post:**
- All posts should have at least 5 pages linking to them
- These links can be from other blog posts and/or landing page FAQs

**Links FROM a post:**
- All posts should link to at least 5 relevant pages
- These links can be to other blog posts and/or landing pages

**For all internal links:**
- Focus on relevance — does the link make sense from a user perspective?
- Anchor text: mix of target keywords and natural, conversational anchors

- Makes content more practical and action-oriented

- How to Convert Powerpoint to Video in 5 Minutes

- Check for updates regularly

### Case Studies
Embed relevant case studies from https://www.synthesia.io/case-studies

### Customer Stories
Short video interviews with real Synthesia customers from YouTube playlist.

### Screenshots
- Must be up to date (latest product UI)
- Sized appropriately
- Use webp format, compressed for page speed
- Include caption and alt text
- Custom screenshots for the specific task preferred

### Highlight Boxes
- Useful for breaking up dense content, adding colour
- Max 100 characters heading, 300 characters text

### First-hand Experience Boxes
- Q&A format with 🌟 emoji
- Highlight first-hand experience from the blog post author
- Format: "Q: [question]" / "A: [personal experience answer]"

### Tables
Custom HTML table component for comparison data.

### Accordions
- Toggle dropdown for supplementary content
- Useful for definitions, technical information without disturbing main flow

### Interactive Synthesia Videos
- Using interactivity features (CTAs, branching, quizzes)
- Still in testing phase

### FAQs
- Generated via AirOps FAQ workflow (being improved)
