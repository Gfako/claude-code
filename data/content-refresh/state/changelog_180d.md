*Description:*
Having released customer-supported email change a few months ago, now it's time for the long-awaited self-serve part.

*What is self-serve email change?*
It’s a helpful addition to the user's profile view that allows them to *change their sign-in email securely without contacting the Customer Success team*.
This feature not only makes account management smoother for users but also improves overall efficiency - we currently process quite a few email change requests manually each month!
With the self-serve option, users can complete the process instantly and securely on their own.

*Why is this feature important?*
An email address is the *primary identifier* for a Synthesia account. It's used for login, password recovery, notifications, and security verification. Over time, users may need to change their email - whether they're switching jobs or schools, consolidating personal accounts, or moving to a new email provider.
Without a self-serve option, this simple change required manual intervention - slowing users down and increasing dependency on Customer Success.
By introducing this feature, we’re empowering users to *stay in control of their account information*, *reduce friction*, and *enhance security* through verified, user-initiated updates.

*Limitations:*
Email change is a security-critical feature. Done wrong, it could lead to serious consequences. To ensure we process requests to the highest security standards, we're limiting this feature to users who created accounts using the email/password sign-up method. This allows us to leverage _Cognito_ (the AWS service responsible for authentication) rather than handling the security-sensitive change and notification process ourselves.

What we shipped :jigsaw:
Videos now automatically retry when they encounter temporary issues, with adjusted spacing between attempts that can handle multi-hour service disruptions. Why it matters :bulb:

Overall Impact:
- ~200 videos automatically recovered across both weeks that would have otherwise failed
- Average of 14% of videos benefited from the new retry mechanism
- Users experienced seamless recovery — no need to restart, contact support, or even know there was a problem

The Big Picture

- Less frustration — users don't see failures for temporary issues
- Fewer support tickets — problems resolve themselves automatically
- Better success rates — especially during transient infrastructure issues
When things go wrong permanently
- Longer time to failure for non-transient issues

*Overview* :fire:
Users now receive real-time script feedback when they open the generate panel, allowing them to fix moderation issues before submitting.
Instead of waiting for asynchronous rejection notifications, users get actionable guidance to resolve script problems and successfully generate their video on the first attempt.

• Reduces user frustration and drop-off from broken flows spanning multiple sessions
*How it works* :gear:
• When you open the generate panel, a new endpoint is called in the background
• If issues are detected, you see clear guidance on what needs to change, before you hit generate
• Fix the flagged content directly in the editor and resubmit without starting over
• This feedback is advisory only, you can still proceed to generate, and normal moderation will happen as usual
