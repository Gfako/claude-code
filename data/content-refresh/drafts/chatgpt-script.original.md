## What is ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is what experts call an "advanced large language model," like a super-smart computer program that understands and uses human language. OpenAI built it on a GPT architecture, which stands for:

- **Generative:** GPT can "generate" new pieces of text. For example, if you give it a story's start, it can keep going and make up the rest.
- **Pre-trained:** This language model was trained to learn from many books, websites, and other types of writing. The "pre-training" helped it learn all sorts of things about language, like grammar and different ways people might say something.
- **Transformer:** This is the method it uses to handle words and sentences. It simultaneously pays attention to the different parts of a sentence to better understand its context and meaning. That's how it makes good guesses about what words might come next or how to answer your question.

You interact with this natural language processing tool by providing prompts/questions. It processes your inputs, generates helpful responses, and can adapt the desired tone and style based on your instructions.

While ChatGPT is particularly popular and known by more than [40%](https://www.statista.com/topics/10446/chatgpt/#topicOverview) of US adults, it's not the only generative AI script writer. Alternatives include Gemini (by Google, previously Bard), Claude, Perplexity AI, and Chatsonic.

ChatGPT surpassed 1 million users in the first five days it became available in November 2022. The latest estimations from 2024 indicate it currently has approximately 180.5 million users worldwide.

## Remember the limitations of ChatGPT video scriptwriting

Like with many other AI tools, ChatGPT's two biggest issues are information accuracy and bias. The scripts you produce will rely exclusively on the setting parameters, meaning the info you give it in your prompt and the data sets used to train ChatGPT.

It's why you'll always see this message in the app, right under the chat bar: " _ChatGPT can make mistakes. Check important info._"

One easy way to avoid accuracy and bias issues is to give ChatGPT better background information when you generate scripts. The right prompt will ensure your scripts are relevant and tailored for successful marketing campaigns.

Detailed prompts will also help you avoid the other three limitations below and any potential shortcomings in the training data.

- **Lack of industry-specific expertise.** With limited specialized knowledge, your video script might sound generic. This can be a drawback when you need detailed content that covers jargon or complex industry-specific details.
- **Inability to incorporate real-time data.** Generated scripts won't include recent events or trends. This can be a significant drawback in fast-moving industries like technology or finance, where updates and innovations occur rapidly, and outdated information can affect your content's relevance.
- **Creative limitations and contextual misunderstandings.** Chat GPT is still learning the art of conversation and might miss the finer points like humor, sarcasm, or that special touch. Only a human writer can grasp the subtleties of the desired tone, style, and emotion.

## 6 Tricks to help you write better video scripts with ChatGPT

ChatGPT doesn't just randomly toss words together — it carefully constructs sentences based on patterns it has learned from a vast amount of text. But it's up to you to make sure it can also grasp the nuances that will make your video script engaging and unique through specific and detailed ChatGPT prompts.

The following script-writing tips will show you how to give clear and precise guidance. In other words, you'll discover how to write the right prompt to bridge the gap between ChatGPT's general AI capabilities and your specific creative vision, as well as how to develop compelling characters.

### 1\. Provide detailed instructions in your prompt

The more you tell ChatGPT about what you need, the better — that's how AI works. Share everything, like who the video is for, what it's about, your style, and what messages you want to include. Be as clear and detailed as possible, and your script will be more likely to match what you're imagining.

### 2\. Give examples of great video scripts

When you stumble across a video that perfectly captures the vibe you're looking for, use it as a launching pad for your video creation. So, tell ChatGPT to create something similar. You won't have that [video's script](https://www.synthesia.io/post/how-to-write-a-video-script), but you can copy its transcript and tell ChatGPT what the video is about, what story it tells, and how, including the unspoken details.

If it's a YouTube video, copy-paste its transcript into ChatGPT. If the transcript is unavailable, use tools like Otter.ai or Descript to extract it and help GPT fully understand what the video is about.

### 3\. Use iterative feedback

Once you get the first draft from ChatGPT, check it out and see what needs to be changed. You might have to tweak the prompt to clarify the desired tone, add more information, change how something is said, or even change the style of the video. Keep adjusting your ChatGPT prompts until the answer feels just right.

### 4\. Incorporate keywords

To make your video pop on search engines or social media marketing campaigns, include some important keywords when you tell ChatGPT what you need. These keywords will make your script relevant and easier to discover.

### 5\. Blend artificial intelligence and human creativity

Let ChatGPT help you come up with the basic ideas and a rough draft, but then add your personal touches. Things like jokes, emotional bits, or creative flair are usually best added by people.

### 6\. Regularly update your prompts

Effective ChatGPT prompts are crucial, but you won't get them right from the start. You might get new ideas or feedback as you work on your project. Make sure to update your instructions to ChatGPT so it can keep helping you as things change. Updates keep the script fresh and consistent with what you need.
