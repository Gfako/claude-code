I assessed each [AI video translator](https://www.synthesia.io/features/video-translator) tool for the following criteria:

- **Accuracy**: how accurate are the subtitles generated? How accurate is the dubbed speech? Does the translation sound natural to a native speaker?
- **Voice cloning**: does the dubbed voice sound like the original person, or is it a robotic-sounding AI voice?
- **Lip-sync**: does the video visually adjust to match the new dubbed voice?
- **Editing features**: is there functionality to edit subtitles, scripts, and correct audio? (AI translations aren't always perfect)
- **Language support**: How many languages can the tool translate into?
- **Ease of use**: Is the interface intuitive and easy to learn?
- **Pricing**: Is the service good value for money?

What are the different ways to translate a video?


When translating videos you have two options:

1. **Subtitles.**
    Translating spoken content into written text displayed on-screen. Adding subtitles to videos allow viewers to read the translation while hearing the original audio. But not everyone loves reading and watching a video at the same time.

2. **Dubbing.**
    Translating and replacing the original audio with a new track in the target language. This can be standard voiceover dubbing, where the video stays the same, or AI lip-sync dubbing, where the video is altered so the speaker’s mouth movements match the translated speech. Poor dubbing can be an unwanted distraction, so quality and accuracy is super important.
