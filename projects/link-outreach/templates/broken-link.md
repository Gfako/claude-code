# Broken Link Email Template

## Context for Claude
When drafting broken link outreach:
- Lead with the problem (broken link on THEIR page) — you're helping them
- Be specific about WHERE the broken link is (section, anchor text)
- Don't make the replacement suggestion feel forced — it should be a natural fit
- Keep under 70 words
- This has the highest response rate of all campaigns because you're solving their problem

## Email Structure

**Subject line:** Examples:
- "Broken link on your {topic} page"
- "Found a dead link on {domain}"
- "Quick heads up — broken link"

**Body:**
```
Hi {first_name},

I was reading your article on {article_topic} and noticed a broken link in the section about {section_context} — it points to {broken_url} which returns a 404.

We have an up-to-date resource on {matching_topic} at {synthesia_url} that covers the same ground. Could work as a replacement.

Either way, wanted to flag it for you.

{sender_name}
```

## Follow-up 1 (3 days later)
```
Hi {first_name},

Just checking if you saw my note about the broken link on your {article_topic} page. The link to {broken_url} is still returning a 404. Happy to help if you need a replacement resource.

{sender_name}
```

## Follow-up 2 (5 days later)
```
Hi {first_name},

Final note on this — the broken link in your {section_context} section is still live. Just wanted to make sure it's on your radar. No worries either way.

{sender_name}
```
